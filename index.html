Featured Article: The Silent Threat

Feral cats in Australia represent one of the most significant conservation challenges of our time. These introduced predators have adapted remarkably well to Australia's diverse environments, from arid deserts to tropical rainforests, and are now found across 99.8% of the continent.

Their hunting efficiency, combined with Australia's unique evolutionary history where native species evolved without placental predators, has created a perfect storm for biodiversity loss. Many of Australia's small mammals, birds, and reptiles have no natural defenses against these skilled hunters.
